# Common Good App — Product Vision

## The problem

Common Good needs one system that supports self-service shopping and the private operational work behind the products.

Typical refill-store workflows rely on employees, handwritten tare weights, disconnected POS systems, spreadsheets, Canva files, supplier bookmarks, and separate recipe notes. Common Good needs these pieces connected without making the customer experience complicated.

## The experience

The technology should feel almost invisible.

A customer should be able to:
- Select a refill product
- Use their own container or a Common Good glass container
- Weigh the product
- Add it to a cart
- Add Pantry Ready products or glass
- See one total
- Pay by the available method
- Leave without needing staff instruction

The owner should be able to:
- Create a product once
- Store its formula, costs, pricing, packaging, labels, suppliers, inventory, and marketing assets together
- Scale batches
- Track manufacturing
- Create accurate on-brand content
- Understand profitability and stock needs

## Core concepts

### Refill
Customer fills a container by weight or volume. Common Good glass has a preloaded tare. Brought-from-home containers are weighed or saved to an optional customer account.

### Pantry Ready
The same core products are packaged and ready to purchase. These support:
- Fast in-store shopping
- First-time trials
- Forgotten containers
- Gifts
- Vendor markets
- Transfer into a permanent glass container at home

### Glass Collection
Curated glass containers, including practical repeatable stock and one-of-a-kind estate-sale pieces. Common Good-provided refill containers are glass-only.

### Product Notebook
The single source of truth for everything about a product.

### Brand Brain
The single source of truth for AI-generated marketing and approved visual assets.

## Success measures

Customer:
- First purchase completed without verbal help
- Few taps and minimal typing
- Correct tare and price
- Clear total and payment instructions

Operations:
- Product costs update reliably
- Recipes scale accurately
- Inventory can be traced
- Labels and approved assets are easy to find
- Market and store inventory stay connected

Brand:
- Images look recognizably Common Good
- Approved labels remain accurate
- No invented product claims or ingredients
